Big Cross Fourth Graders Win Regional Recognition in Special Olympics Inclusion Contest

Big Cross Fourth Graders Win Regional Recognition in Special Olympics Inclusion Contest
Apr 14 2025

Students at Big Cross Street School are celebrating a big win for inclusion and creativity. The fourth-grade class taught by Amanda Burt and Lindsey St. Clair earned third place out of 79 regional entries in the Special Olympics New York “Opening Doors for Inclusion” door decorating contest.

Their entry, inspired by the Disney movie Lilo and Stitch, featured the heartfelt quote: “Ohana means family. And family means no one gets left behind or forgotten.” The beautifully decorated door served as a vibrant reminder of the importance of unity and acceptance for all students. 

As a result of their win, Big Cross will now proudly display a Unified Champions banner, a symbol of the school's commitment to inclusion and the values championed by Special Olympics New York.

Several other classrooms across the district joined the effort and created thoughtful entries of their own. Participating groups included Mary Collier and Nicole Matino’s classes at the middle school, Jared Halliday’s class at the high school, and Kim Lewis’s No Place for Hate club at Big Cross. Way to include!
