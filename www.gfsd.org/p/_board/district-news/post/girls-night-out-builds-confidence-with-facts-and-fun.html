Girls' Night Out builds confidence with facts and fun

Girls' Night Out builds confidence with facts and fun
Apr 1 2025

"By being here tonight, you are committing to a more inspired, connected and empowered version of yourself," Pam Fisher told more than 100 young women gathered for Girls Night Out at Glens Falls High School. "A more secure you is not only for yourself, but also you become a stronger support to the women around you. All you’ll need to hear is your inner, authentic voice."

The annual event organized by the High School's SoroptiMISS Club is an initiative designed to strengthen and empower high school girls with life skills, information, and bonding over fun activities. Students Claire Fisher, Chloe Wright, and Isabelle Aurielia led the organizing committee: Piper Dock, Ella Harris, Lilliam Wright, Sylvia Slingerland, Josie Hogan, Lila Hubany, Allison McCoy, Isabelle Nzambi, Zoey Moon, Sofia Rice, Norah Shafferand, and Paige VanSchaick.

Girls were first treated to an enormous buffet of fettuccine alfredo made by culinary students Keegan Howe, Kellen Burns, Josiah Newman-Moron and Tim Barody, baked ziti and meatballs made by Mr. Petrillose's culinary classes, salads, pizza, and other goodies. 

Mrs. Fisher, MBA, Director of External Affairs at Hudson Headwaters Health Network, delivered an honest and inspiring talk about her hearing journey, which eventually led to cochlear implants.

"What I can tell you is that I only knew what I knew," Mrs. Fisher said. "What I mean by that is that our knowledge and experience is limited to our own little corners of the world. But we can seek to know more. As I tell my daughter, Claire, all the time... 'Nothing changes if nothing changes.'"

As the girls listened intently, she continued. "As the years have passed, slowly I began sharing my story. And I realized how strong I am as a writer, how quickly I catch on to emotional cues. How I notice when greater accessibility is needed, and the power of empathy. My circumstances make me a better leader. And slowly, so much weight fell off my shoulders, and so much more energy became available to me, energy that was once applied to hiding who I was." 

"My deepest wish for you is that if there is an area of your life that feels inauthentic, think about your story," urged Mrs. Fisher. "Hearing was mine. Can you turn what might seem like a problem into power and purpose?"

Inspired girls then rotated through several workshops, including:

  • An active approach to personal safety through practical self-defense techniques, teaching situational awareness, personal protection, and empowerment through preparedness. (Facilitated by Derek Ellis & Dijah)
     
  • Cutting specified patterns in Tyvek, a waterproof material that will be used to make reusable menstrual pads with the MoonCatcher Project. These pads help to keep girls in school throughout the world. (Facilitated by Randi Broadwell of MoonBee)
     
  • Practicing a fun journey through calming and energizing Yoga flows that sharpens the mind, balances emotions, and releases stress. (Facilitated by Nicole Mantilla)
     
  • Creating their own lip balm using locally made beeswax and honey by melting, scenting, and pouring their own chapstick to take home. (Facilitated by Mrs. Nicole McGrath)
     
  • Making a pressed flower “spring themed” votive candle holder with electric tea light. (Facilitated by Stephanie Schneider)
     
  • Pampering with colored hair extensions and manicures from BOCES cosmetology students. (Facilitated by Asenath Bryant)
     
  • Creating unique crafts, including bracelets using many different charms and colored beads (facilitated by Mrs. Christine Delsignore), tote bags and canvas pouches with paint, glitter and embellishments (facilitated by Mrs. Susanne Scarincio), tie-dyeing shirts (facilitated by Hannah Harrington and Mrs. Kristy Walkup), folding jumping frog origami patterns (facilitated by Mrs. Jennifer Girard), and beautiful paintings or bookmarks (facilitated by Ms.  Ziegler, Mrs. Hjelmar, & Mx. Kent).
     
  • Potting succulent plants as a calm, mindful activity that can help reduce stress and bring some green into girls’ homes (facilitated by Ms. DeLong).
     
  • Notecard-making with colorful stamps and paper decoration (facilitated by Mrs. Mary Satterfield and Mrs. Joanne Hurlburt).

Many thanks to the sponsors of Girls Night Out 2025: T3 Coaching (Kevin Crossman), Della Auto Group, Coastal Society, Angelina’s Pizza, Cooper’s Cave, West Mountain, Chris Nolan, Matt Petrillose, Alice Crotty, Sky Zone, Sandy Norris, Dot Clarke, Allison Sharrow, Assured Home Inspections, Mediation Services - Elizabeth Little, Esq., Adirondack Cardiology - Robert Hogan, Jackie Hogan, Sunflower, nePromo, Salt Cave, Candy Space, Just Water, Minky Mink, and Ella Harris.
